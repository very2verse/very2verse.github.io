1.[**nascent**](https://dictionary.cambridge.org/dictionary/english/nascent): an #adjective that describes something—such as an idea, industry, or relationship—that has recently come into existence and is just beginning to develop, with the potential to grow larger or stronger.

2.[**tantamount**](https://dictionary.cambridge.org/dictionary/english/tantamount?st_source=ai_mode): means equivalent in value, meaning, or effect. It is almost exclusively used in the phrase "tantamount to" to describe an action or situation that is effectively the same as another, even if it is not technically identical. #adjective

3.[**to blow a raspberry**](https://www.collinsdictionary.com/dictionary/english/to-blow-a-raspberry): a #phrase that describes the act of sticking your tongue between your lips and blowing to create a wet, buzzing sound that mimics flatulence.

4.[**acquiesce**](https://www.merriam-webster.com/dictionary/acquiesce):to accept, agree, or give in to something without protest, often doing so reluctantly or passively / to accept, agree, or comply usually by staying silent or by not arguing (often used with to or in) #verb
